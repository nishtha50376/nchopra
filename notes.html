Learning about THz-Time Domain Spectroscopy (THz-TDS) system components:

Key Components in THz-TDS

In a THz-TDS system, the femtosecond laser and delay line are often the most underrated components, yet they are crucial. The femtosecond laser serves as the main excitation sub-system, producing ultra-short optical pulses that generate the broadband, coherent THz radiation essential for time-domain measurements. Without it, achieving a functional THz-TDS system would be challenging.

The delay line acts like the "clock" of the THz system. It allows precise time-delay control between the THz pulse and detection window, enabling the reconstruction of the entire THz pulse waveform in the time domain.
